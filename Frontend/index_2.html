Judge FAQ

The hard questions,
answered honestly.

Why not just use GPT-4?
GPT-4 takes ~2s, costs $0.04–0.12 per query, requires reliable internet, and knows nothing about Punjabi agricultural slang. KisanCall runs in <500ms, costs a fraction of a cent, and has been explicitly fine-tuned on agri vocabulary. At 10M farmer queries/month, GPT-4 costs ₹30 crore. KisanCall costs ₹15 lakh.
Do real farmers have smartphones?
That's the point — they don't need one. KisanCall's roadmap includes IVR integration so any farmer can dial a toll-free number from a ₹800 feature phone and get the same service. The web demo is for judges; the product is for a 2G voice call.
What languages are supported?
Punjabi (Gurmukhi script), Hindi (Devanagari), and Romanized transliterations of both — because that's how STT output often looks ("kanak di bimari" instead of "ਕਣਕ ਦੀ ਬਿਮਾਰੀ"). The guardrails model handles all three forms.
How accurate is the STT really?
Baseline Whisper-small hits 28% WER on Punjabi. After fine-tuning on Common Voice + synthetic agri phrases, we achieve 16% WER — a 43% reduction. On agricultural domain terms specifically, we improved from 35% to 11% WER.
Why SLMs instead of one big model?
Each model in the cascade is an expert at one narrow task: transcribe → filter → classify → respond. This is faster (parallelizable), cheaper to fine-tune, easier to update, and allows edge deployment. Replacing one model doesn't break the others.
Is this production-ready?
The STT and Guardrails modules are fully functional and demonstrated here. Intent Router and Response Generator are the next 30-day milestone. The architecture, pipeline, and training code are production-grade — not a notebook hack.
